Modular Grid Mark

A 3×3 block grid where the active cells trace a hidden K in negative space. Hollow cells read as inactive modules. Solid cells read as enabled features.

The gold block occupies the top-right position — the only color deviation. It marks the authority layer: the master tenant, the administrative apex. It never appears on hollow cells.

  • Grid unit 28px · Gap 6px · Radius 3px
  • Hollow cells = 18% opacity stroke only
  • Gold accent = Master / Owner tier
  • Hidden K: Col 1 full, Col 2 mid-row, Col 3 top+bottom
  • Wordmark: Bebas Neue, tracking +6px
  • Sub: DM Mono, all caps, tracking +3px

Fortress Badge

A bastion form — taken from the geometry of Fort Charlotte: a downward-tapered pentagon with merlon notches cut from the upper corners. The shape reads as a shield but was never designed as one.

The mini grid mark lives at the center. This badge is never the primary logo — it marks secure surfaces, audit-ready features, and compliance documentation.

  • Use on: compliance certificates, data agreements
  • Use on: admin dashboards, security settings
  • Use on: "Powered by KRM" white-label footers
  • Never: as a primary logo on outward-facing collateral
  • Gold variant reserved for Kemis master tier only

KRM Monogram

K and R are constructed from identical stroke widths. The K diagonal arms are angular — no curves. The R bump uses a single controlled arc, the only curve in the system.

The M is deliberately understated: a gold vertical accent on the right edge. It reads as an M to those who look, as a color accent to those who don't.

  • At 16px: M drops, KR reads cleanly
  • At 32px+: Gold M accent visible
  • Grid Mark favours 32px+ for legibility
  • Both marks work at 16px — test both

Gold Usage Protocol
Gold appears on: top-right block in the grid mark, master-tier badge, footnote rules, and compliance stamps. It is never used for body text or interactive UI elements.
Gold communicates: Kemis ownership, master tenant hierarchy, auditable trust. Overuse collapses its meaning into decoration. It must be sparse to stay sovereign.
